Lanciano, 750 A.D.

← Back to Miracles

The Eucharistic Miracle of Lanciano is widely considered the oldest recorded Eucharistic miracle in the Catholic Church. Around 750 A.D., a monk experiencing doubts saw the consecrated bread and wine transform into living human flesh and blood.

The Transformed Relics

The Host: Transformed into a rounded membrane of light-brown human flesh.
The Wine: Transformed into liquid blood, which later coagulated into five distinct, unequal pellets.

Scientific Investigations

  • Cardiac Tissue: Examination by Dr. Odoardo Linoli (1970-71) identified the flesh as striated muscular tissue from a human heart, specifically the left ventricle.
  • Blood Type: Both the flesh and blood samples are human AB blood type, matching the type found on the Shroud of Turin.
  • Zero Preservatives: Extensive tests confirmed that no chemical or embalming substances were ever used to preserve these tissues, despite being exposed to elements for over 1,200 years.

Location

The relics are preserved under the custody of the Conventual Friars Minor at the Sanctuary of the Eucharistic Miracle (Church of St. Francis) in Lanciano, Italy.
