Bordeaux, 1822

← Back to Miracles

The Eucharistic Miracle of Bordeaux occurred on Septuagesima Sunday, 3 February 1822, in the chapel of the newly founded Association of the Holy Family on Rue Mazarin in Bordeaux, France. During a standard benediction service, a striking 20-minute visual apparition of Jesus Christ replaced the appearance of the consecrated host inside the monstrance.

The Event & Testimony

  • The Priest: Abbot Delort was substituting for the community's founder, Venerable Father Pierre-Bienvenu Noailles, to give the Benediction of the Blessed Sacrament.
  • The Vision: As soon as the host was exposed in the monstrance, witnesses saw the head and upper body of a remarkably beautiful young man, approximately 30 years old, with fair shoulder-length hair. He wore a dark red scarf draped across his shoulder and chest. His left hand rested on his heart, while his right hand was extended outward to bless the congregation.
  • The Movement: The apparition appeared completely alive, smiling sweetly and bowing his head from side to side during the 20 minutes of prayer.
  • Auditory Miracle: A parishioner named Milady Peychaud did not see the figure but clearly heard a voice in her heart state, "I am He who is. Only I am.".

Church Investigation & Legacy

Following the event, the Archbishop of Bordeaux deployed a panel of theologians and scientists to thoroughly interview the 21 people present in the chapel. Fourteen formal testimonies were gathered, yielding completely consistent details. Satisfied with the evidence, the Archbishop declared the event an authentic miracle. Shortly thereafter, Pope Leo XII affirmed the miracle and signed a papal brief establishing the Feast of the Holy Family to commemorate it.
