Mogoro, 1604

← Back to Miracles

The Eucharistic Miracle of Mogoro occurred on Easter Monday, 1604, in the town of Mogoro, Sardinia. It is a unique miracle where consecrated Hosts left permanent, perfect impressions on stone.

The Event

During Mass, two men in a state of mortal sin received the Eucharist. Upon receiving the Hosts, they felt them turn into burning embers and spat them out onto the altar stone. When the pastor, Father Salvatore Spiga, cleaned the area, he discovered that the Hosts had left two perfect, distinct circular imprints deeply stamped into the stone floor.

Historical Proof & Preservation

Historical Record: A public notary act written by Pedro Antonio Escano on May 25, 1686, provides legal and historical validation for the miracle, detailing the construction of a tabernacle to house the "stone of the miracle."

Legacy: The stone bearing the twin imprints is still visible today. Every year on Divine Mercy Sunday, the town holds a solemn Eucharistic procession to commemorate this event.
