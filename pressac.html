Pressac, 1643

← Back to Miracles

The Eucharistic Miracle of Pressac (France) occurred on Holy Thursday in 1643. A devastating fire broke out in the parish church, melting the chalice entirely. Miraculously, the consecrated Host remained completely intact, protected under a bubble of the melted tin from the chalice's base.

The Event

  • The Fire: On April 16, 1643, a severe fire ignited in the parish church of Pressac. The flames completely consumed the repository and melted the tin chalice holding the consecrated Host.
  • The Miracle: Amidst the ashes and the melted metal, only the foot of the chalice survived. The heat had formed a bubble of tin on the metal base, and when it was examined, the Host was found inside—completely untouched by the flames and perfectly intact.
  • The Aftermath: The vicar, Simon Sauvage, recovered the Host (which had turned a light red around the edges) and moved it to the main altar. The following morning, on Good Friday, the miraculous Host was reverently consumed during Mass.

Documentation and Veneration

Official Recognition: The Abbot of Availles-Limouzine, François du Theil, documented testimonies of the event and delivered them to Henri Louis Chastagnier de la Roche-Posay, the Bishop of Poitiers. The Bishop subsequently authorized the veneration of the miracle.

Historical Evidence: The events were well-documented by eyewitness accounts and are still commemorated today in the stained-glass windows of the Pressac church, which visually depict the different phases of the miracle.
