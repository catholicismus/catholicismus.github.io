Marseille-en-Beauvaisis, 1533

← Back to Miracles

The 1533 Eucharistic Miracle of Marseille-en-Beauvaisis (France) occurred when thieves stole a silver ciborium and consecrated Hosts from the local parish church, discarding the Hosts under a rock in a field. Despite a severe snowstorm, the Hosts were found the next day completely intact and without any snow on the rock that protected them.

The Miracle and Devotion

Following their miraculous recovery, numerous healings were reported at the site, which led to a surge of popular devotion and the eventual building of the Chapel of the Sacred Hosts.

Historical Documentation

Despite the profound faith and devotion the event inspired, the Hosts were ultimately destroyed by individuals seeking to profane them. The historical details are documented in works like the Histoire de la Ville de Beauvais. Today, the memory of this event is preserved by the Catholic Church as part of the recognized worldwide eucharistic miracles.
