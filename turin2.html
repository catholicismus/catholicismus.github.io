Turin, 1640

← Back to Miracles

The 1640 Eucharistic Miracle of Turin occurred at the Church of Santa Maria del Monte during the Siege of Turin, when a miraculous blast of fire from the tabernacle stopped soldiers from desecrating the Holy Eucharist.

The Event

During the chaos of the Siege of Turin, French soldiers invaded the church. When a soldier pried open the tabernacle to seize the consecrated Hosts, a sudden, powerful blast of fire erupted from the ciborium, enveloping and severely burning him. This divine intervention terrified the soldiers, instantly halting the violence and saving the church from further destruction.

Legacy

The event was documented by eyewitness Capuchin Friar Pier Maria da Cambiano. The tabernacle door, which survived the blast, bears what is believed to be the scorched handprint left by the soldier. This miracle serves as a testament to the sanctity of the Real Presence, standing alongside Turin’s earlier 1453 Eucharistic miracle.
