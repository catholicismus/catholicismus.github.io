Morne-Rouge, 1902

← Back to Miracles

The 1902 Eucharistic Miracle of Morne-Rouge occurred in Martinique during the catastrophic eruption of Mount Pelée, when parishioners witnessed a vision of the Sacred Heart of Jesus within the consecrated Host, offering them spiritual mercy in their final hours.

The Event

As volcanic destruction approached on Ascension Day, the villagers of Morne-Rouge gathered in their church. During Eucharistic adoration, the congregation witnessed the image of Jesus, with His Sacred Heart crowned with thorns, appearing within the Host. Many reported seeing blood dripping from His heart. This vision lasted for several hours while the village was miraculously spared from the initial lava flow.

Significance

While the village was later destroyed in a subsequent eruption, Catholic tradition views this miracle as a merciful act of God, allowing the people to reconcile their relationships and prepare their souls for eternity. This event is officially cataloged in the Vatican-approved exhibition of Eucharistic Miracles.
