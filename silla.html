Silla, 1907

← Back to Miracles

The 1907 Eucharistic miracle of Silla, Spain, is marked by the miraculous, incorrupt preservation of consecrated Hosts that were stolen and abandoned in a field, remaining perfectly intact for over a century.

The Theft and Miracle

In 1907, the tabernacle of the Church of Our Lady of the Angels was forced open and the ciborium stolen. Two days later, the Hosts were found hidden under a stone in a nearby orchard. Despite the passage of decades, the Hosts showed no signs of decay, leading to formal ecclesiastical investigations in 1934 and 1982 to verify their supernatural state of preservation.

Legacy

The miraculous Hosts remain preserved and available for public veneration in the Church of Our Lady of the Angels in Silla. They stand as a silent yet powerful testament to Christ’s enduring presence in the Eucharist, even when faced with human malice and the passage of time.
