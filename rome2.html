Rome, 1610

← Back to Miracles

The 1610 Eucharistic Miracle of Rome occurred at the Basilica of Santa Pudenziana when a priest, struggling with doubts about the Real Presence, dropped a consecrated Host which left a permanent imprint and bloodstain on the marble steps.

The Event

During Holy Mass, a priest experiencing internal doubts accidentally dropped a consecrated Host. Upon hitting the marble floor, the Host left a visible indentation and a permanent bloodstain, serving as a miraculous testament to the reality of transubstantiation.

Preservation & Site

Current Location: The miraculous imprint remains preserved within the Caetani Chapel inside the Basilica of Santa Pudenziana.

Historical Context: Built in 145 AD, the Basilica is one of the oldest Christian worship sites in Rome, reportedly constructed over the home of the nobleman Senator Pudens, who once hosted St. Peter the Apostle.
