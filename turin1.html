Turin, 1453

← Back to Miracles

The 1453 Eucharistic Miracle of Turin, Italy, saw a stolen consecrated Host levitate above a city square before descending into a bishop's chalice, a profound event captured in the city's historical records.

The Miraculous Event

After a soldier stole a monstrance containing a consecrated Host, his mule fell in a Turin city square, spilling the sacred items. To the astonishment of the gathered crowd, the Host rose into the air and began to levitate. Bishop Ludovico da Romagnano, arriving with a golden chalice, prayed as the Host miraculously descended from the air directly into the vessel.

Legacy and Commemoration

The event is documented in local records from 1454. The Basilica of Corpus Domini was built on the site to honor the miracle, with an iron railing marking the exact spot where the mule had collapsed. The original chalice used in the event remains preserved within the Basilica.
