Rimini, 1227

← Back to Miracles

The 1227 Eucharistic Miracle of Rimini is a historical event where a starving mule knelt in adoration before the Holy Eucharist, bypassing a fresh bowl of oats. Performed by Saint Anthony of Padua, this miracle proved the Real Presence of Christ.

The Challenge

A heretic named Bonovillo denied the Real Presence. He challenged Saint Anthony to a test: he would starve his mule for three days, then present it with food on one side and the Blessed Sacrament on the other. If the mule ignored the food to kneel before the Host, Bonovillo vowed to convert.

The Miracle at Piazza Grande

On the day of the test, the mule was brought to the public square. Despite being famished, the animal ignored the barley offered by its master. It walked past the food, knelt before the Blessed Sacrament held by Saint Anthony, and bowed in reverence. This miraculous act led to Bonovillo’s immediate conversion.

Legacy

Due to his success in defending Church doctrine, Saint Anthony became known as the "Hammer of Heretics." Today, a small octagonal shrine stands at the Piazza Tre Martiri in Rimini, marking the exact site of this miracle.
