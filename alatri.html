Alatri, 1228

← Back to Miracles

The 1228 Eucharistic miracle in Alatri, Italy, involved a consecrated Host that transformed into bleeding flesh after a young woman stole it at the bidding of a sorceress to use in a love potion.

The Backstory

The Theft: Seeking to regain the affections of her former fiancé, a young woman consulted an older sorceress. The sorceress instructed her to receive a consecrated Host during Mass, conceal it in a cloth, and bring it to her.

The Transformation: After hiding the stolen Host for three days, the woman went to retrieve it. She discovered that the bread had visibly transformed into a piece of bleeding human flesh.

The Aftermath and Resolution

Overcome with guilt and fear, the young woman confessed her sacrilegious act to the local bishop. The bishop wrote to Pope Gregory IX for guidance. In 1228, the Pope issued the papal bull Fraternitas Tuae, which officially documented the miracle and granted pardon to the repentant woman.

The Relic Today

The relic of the flesh is still preserved and displayed today inside a reliquary at the Cathedral of St. Paul the Apostle in Alatri, Italy.
