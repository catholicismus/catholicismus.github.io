Seefeld, 1384

← Back to Miracles

The 1384 Eucharistic Miracle of Seefeld, Austria, occurred when a local, arrogant knight, Oswald Milser, demanded to receive a large Host reserved only for priests. As he unworthily received it, the ground beneath him gave way, and the Host began to bleed, profoundly humbling the knight.

The Timeline & Events

  • Holy Thursday (1384): Knight Oswald Milser, known for his tyranny and demanding ego, came to Mass with his armed men and forced the terrified priest to give him the large Host.
  • The Sinking: Upon taking the Host in his mouth, Milser immediately sank to his knees, and the solid stone floor beneath him softened like quicksand. To keep himself from falling into the ground, he grabbed the stone altar, causing it to melt under his fingers and leaving deep physical imprints.
  • The Bleeding: Terrified, the knight begged the priest to remove the Host. When the priest retrieved it, the Host was observed to have turned bright red and was actively dripping blood.

Aftermath & Legacy

Penance: The knight’s arrogance dissolved into deep repentance. He confessed his sacrilege at the nearby Stams Monastery and spent the remaining two years of his life performing penance.

Physical Evidence: The miraculous, blood-stained Host was initially divided, with a portion placed in the local Seefeld parish. The melted altar stone and floor imprints remain visible to this day.

The Pilgrimage Site: Because of the event's widespread validation, Seefeld quickly became a famous Austrian pilgrimage site. The Church of St. Oswald—where the relic is displayed—continues to draw thousands of visitors.
