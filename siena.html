Siena, 1730

← Back to Miracles

The 1730 Eucharistic Miracle of Siena, Italy, involves the miraculous, incorrupt preservation of 351 consecrated Hosts stolen from the Basilica of San Francesco. Centuries later, these Hosts remain as fresh as the day they were baked, defying all natural laws of biological decay.

The Theft and Discovery

On August 14, 1730, thieves stole a ciborium containing 351 Hosts. Three days later, they were found discarded in an offering box at the Sanctuary of Santa Maria in Provenzano. Despite being covered in dirt and dust, the Hosts remained intact and were eventually brought back to the Basilica.

Scientific Validation

Scientific examinations in 1914, 1922, 1950, and 1951 confirmed that the Hosts are completely uncorrupted. A 1789 control test proved that ordinary bread rots under identical conditions, whereas these consecrated Hosts remained pristine, leading scientists to conclude that their preservation is inexplicable by natural agents.

Current Status

Today, 223 of the original Hosts are kept in the Basilica of San Francesco. During a visit in 1980, Pope St. John Paul II knelt before them and declared, "It is the Presence."
