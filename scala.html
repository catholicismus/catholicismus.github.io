Scala, 1732

← Back to Miracles

The 1732 Eucharistic Miracle of Scala, Italy, saw the miraculous appearance of the symbols of Christ’s Passion within consecrated Hosts over a period of three months at the Monastery of the Most Holy Redeemer.

The Manifestation

Starting on September 11, 1732, during the weekly Thursday exposition of the Blessed Sacrament, witnesses observed vivid symbols of Christ's suffering forming inside the Host. These included a black cross, the crown of thorns, the spear, the sponge, and the pillars of the scourging.

Validation and Records

Direct Witness: The great Doctor of the Church, St. Alphonsus Liguori, was a direct witness to the manifestations and confirmed their validity.

Official Record: The phenomenon was meticulously documented by Bishop Santoro of Scala in a report sent to the Apostolic Nuncio of Naples and subsequently forwarded to the Vatican. It remains a recognized historical miracle cataloged by Blessed Carlo Acutis.
