Santarém, 1247

← Back to Miracles

The 1247 Eucharistic miracle of Santarém, Portugal, is one of the most venerated miracles in Church history. It involves a consecrated Host that bled profusely after being stolen and hidden, later manifesting a miraculous crystal ampoule formation.

The Miracle

A woman stole a consecrated Host to use in a sorcery spell. Upon leaving the church, the Host began to bleed. Terrified, she hid it in a trunk, which later emitted mysterious, brilliant light. The Host was recovered and returned to the Church of St. Stephen, where it continued to bleed for three days.

Legacy

In 1340, the wax protecting the Host shattered, and the Host was found miraculously sealed within a transparent crystal ampoule. Scientific analysis in 1997 confirmed the tissue remains intact. Today, the Church of the Holy Miracle is a major pilgrimage site, drawing thousands annually to venerate the relic and commemorate God's merciful presence.
