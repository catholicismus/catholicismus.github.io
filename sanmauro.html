San Mauro La Bruca, 1969

← Back to Miracles

The 1969 Eucharistic Miracle of San Mauro La Bruca refers to the perfect, uncorrupted preservation of 63 consecrated Communion hosts following a church robbery.

The Event

On July 25, 1969, thieves broke into the parish church in Salerno, Italy, and stole a ciborium containing 63 consecrated hosts. They emptied the ciborium onto a nearby path. The following morning, a child discovered the hosts, and they were returned to the parish priest.

Scientific and Church Recognition

  • Natural Decomposition: Under normal circumstances, unleavened bread begins to degrade within six months and turns to dust within a few years.
  • Scientific Analysis: Chemical evaluations conducted over several decades confirmed that the hosts showed zero signs of decay, remaining structurally intact.
  • Official Recognition: In 1994, after 25 years of monitoring, Bishop Biagio d'Agostino officially recognized the phenomenon as a miracle and approved the public veneration of the hosts.
