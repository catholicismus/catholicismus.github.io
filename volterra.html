Volterra, 1472

← Back to Miracles

The 1472 Eucharistic Miracle of Volterra, Italy, occurred during the tumultuous "Allumiere War" when a soldier's attempt to desecrate a stolen ciborium resulted in the Hosts levitating in the air, surrounded by unearthly light.

The Miraculous Event

A Florentine soldier, after plundering the Cathedral of Volterra, hurled a stolen ivory ciborium against a wall in a fit of rage. As the Hosts spilled out, instead of hitting the ground, they miraculously elevated into the air and remained suspended, emitting a brilliant light. This sight so terrified the soldier that he immediately fell to his knees in repentance.

Historical Legacy

The incident took place during the sacking of Volterra, and the event was documented by numerous eyewitnesses, including Friar Biagio Lisci. The miracle serves as a powerful historical reminder of the divine protection of the Blessed Sacrament even in times of extreme human conflict and destruction.
