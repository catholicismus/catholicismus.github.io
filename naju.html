Naju, South Korea (1985)

← Back to Miracles

The reported phenomena in Naju, South Korea, are controversial events centered around a visionary named Julia Kim that began in the 1980s. While they attracted international attention, the Catholic Church has officially rejected the supernatural nature of these events.

Reported Phenomena

  • Host Transformation: Claims that the Eucharist transformed into human flesh and blood on the visionary's tongue.
  • Miraculous Descent: Reports of sacred hosts appearing out of thin air.
  • Weeping Statue: Claims that a statue of the Virgin Mary wept real tears and tears of blood.

Official Church Standing

Not Supernatural: The Dicastery for the Doctrine of the Faith issued a ruling of non constat de supernaturalitate, meaning the supernatural character of these events is not confirmed.

Strict Prohibitions: The local Archdiocese of Gwangju and the Catholic Bishops' Conference of Korea have officially banned Catholics from visiting the site. Participating in these unauthorized movements carries severe spiritual penalties within the Church.
