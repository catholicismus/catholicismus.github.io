Sant Joan de les Abadesses, 1251

← Back to Miracles

The 1251 Eucharistic miracle of Sant Joan de les Abadesses, Spain, involved a consecrated Host hidden within a cavity in a wooden crucifix, which remained miraculously incorrupt for over 175 years until its accidental discovery in 1426.

The Hidden Eucharist

In 1251, a consecrated Host was wrapped in linen and sealed inside the forehead of a large wooden crucifix. The memory of the hidden Host was lost to time until restoration works in 1426 revealed a small silver plaque sealing the statue's forehead. Upon opening, the Host was found in a state of perfect preservation, showing no signs of natural decay despite nearly two centuries of concealment.

Legacy

Known locally as "The Most Holy Mystery of Saint John of the Abbesses," the relic continues to be housed in the historic monastery. It serves as a profound symbol of the enduring presence of Christ, remaining an object of pilgrimage for the faithful who travel to venerate this centuries-old miracle.
