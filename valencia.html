Valencia, Spain

← Back to Miracles

The Archdiocese of Valencia, Spain, is home to several profound Eucharistic legacies, including the 1348 "Miracle of the Fish," the incorrupt Hosts of Silla, and the revered Holy Chalice, traditionally considered the Grail of the Last Supper.

Key Eucharistic Traditions

  • Miracle of the Fish (1348): After three Hosts were swept away in a flood, fishermen discovered them intact in the mouths of three fish, which returned them to the priest's chalice.
  • Miracle of Silla (1907): Consecrated Hosts stolen from a parish church were found in a garden, remaining perfectly incorrupt for over a century.
  • The Holy Chalice (Santo Cáliz): Housed in the Valencia Cathedral, this ancient vessel is widely regarded as the cup used by Jesus during the Last Supper.

Legacy

The region’s Eucharistic devotion is celebrated annually through the world’s largest monstrance, the "Monstrance of the Poor," which features detailed carvings of these miraculous events, continuing the tradition of faith and wonder in the Valencian Church.
