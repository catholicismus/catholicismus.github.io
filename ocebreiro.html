O'Cebreiro, 1300

← Back to Miracles

The 1300 Eucharistic miracle of O'Cebreiro, Spain, is a renowned event where a priest's lack of faith was resolved when the Host and wine transformed into real flesh and blood during a winter blizzard.

The Miracle

During a severe blizzard, a pious peasant named Juan Santín walked to church, only to be mocked by a skeptical priest. At the consecration, the Host transformed into flesh and the wine into blood, soaking the altar cloth. Notably, a statue of the Virgin Mary bowed in adoration, confirming the Real Presence of Christ.

Legacy

The chalice used during this event is famously known as the "Galician Holy Grail" and is depicted on the coat of arms of the Kingdom of Galicia. The relics are still venerated today in the Santa María la Real church, serving as a testament to the faith that sustains pilgrims along the Camino de Santiago.
