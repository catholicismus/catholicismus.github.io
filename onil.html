Onil, 1824

← Back to Miracles

The 1824 Eucharistic miracle of Onil, Spain, is celebrated for the miraculous, permanent preservation of a consecrated Host that was stolen and left in an open field, remaining incorrupt for over two centuries.

The Theft and Recovery

In November 1824, a man named Nicolás Bernabeu stole a monstrance containing the Blessed Sacrament from the parish church. After the thief was apprehended, the stolen monstrance was discovered in a field weeks later. To the astonishment of all, the Host inside remained perfectly intact, despite the passage of time and exposure to the elements.

Legacy

The authenticity of the miracle was officially verified by the Archdiocese of Valencia in 1943. Known locally as "Nostre Senyor Robat" (The Stolen Lord of Onil), the miraculous Host is venerated today in the Church of Saint James the Apostle, serving as a profound witness to Christ's enduring presence.
