Moncada, 1392

← Back to Miracles

The 1392 Eucharistic miracle of Moncada, Spain, occurred when a five-year-old girl named Inés (later Saint Inés de Moncada) confirmed the Real Presence of Jesus in the Host, thereby resolving a priest's severe spiritual crisis regarding the validity of his priesthood.

The Miracle of Confirmation

Amidst the confusion of the Western Schism, Father Mosén Jaime Carrós suffered from deep agony regarding his priestly validity. During Christmas Mass, young Inés witnessed the Baby Jesus in his hands during the consecration. To test her, the priest later showed her both a consecrated and an unconsecrated host; she correctly identified Jesus only in the consecrated one, providing the divine confirmation the priest desperately needed.

Legacy

This event not only restored the priest's faith but also laid the foundation for the sanctity of little Inés. The miracle is historically documented in the city archives of Moncada, serving as a reminder that God’s grace works through the humble and the pure of heart.
