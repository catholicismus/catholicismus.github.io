Boxtel-Hoogstraten, 1380

← Back to Miracles

The Eucharistic Miracle of Boxtel-Hoogstraten (Netherlands) occurred around 1380 when a priest, Fr. Eligius van der Aker, accidentally knocked over a chalice containing consecrated white wine. The wine visibly transformed into blood, staining both the corporal and the altar cloth.

Relics and Locations

The relics of the miracle were divided and are preserved in two locations:

  • The Blood-stained Corporal: Kept in Boxtel.
  • The Altar Cloth: Given to the town of Hoogstraten.

Key Details & Historical Context

  • The Incident: During a Mass at the altar of the Three Kings at St. Peter's Church, the consecrated wine spilled, immediately turning into blood.
  • The Investigation: The most authoritative document confirming the miracle is an official decree issued in 1380 by Cardinal Pileus, who investigated the event.
  • Celebrations: The event is officially recognized by the Catholic Church and is still honored with large processions and celebrations.
