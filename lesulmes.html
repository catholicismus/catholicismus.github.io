Les Ulmes, 1668

← Back to Miracles

On June 2, 1668, during Eucharistic adoration in Les Ulmes, France, a glowing apparition of Christ appeared inside the exposed Host. Witnesses saw a figure with light brown hair, crossed hands, and a white tunic for about 15 minutes before it vanished, leaving the Host in its original state.

Event Timeline and Details

  • The Date & Location: June 2, 1668 (Saturday during the octave of Corpus Christi), in the small parish church of Les Ulmes, France.
  • The Vision: During public exposition of the Blessed Sacrament, the Host began to radiate bright light. A miraculous vision of Jesus Christ appeared. The apparition had a luminous face, light brown hair falling over His back, and His hands crossed on His chest while wearing a white tunic.
  • Duration: The vision lasted for roughly 15 minutes before being enveloped by a small, luminous cloud that gradually dissipated, leaving the Host as it was before.

Investigation and Legacy

Church Approval: On June 13, the local pastor notified Bishop Henry Arnauld of Angers. The Bishop immediately launched a thorough investigation and subsequently authorized devotion to the miracle on June 25, 1668.

The Miraculous Host: The Host was kept in a special recess in the church for over 130 years. During the French Revolution, the Vicar of Puy-Notre-Dame devoutly consumed the Host to prevent it from being desecrated by anti-clerical forces.

Present Day: The recess that historically contained the miraculous Host is still visible in the church in Les Ulmes.
