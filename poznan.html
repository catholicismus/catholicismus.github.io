Poznań, 1399

← Back to Miracles

The 1399 Eucharistic Miracle of Poznań, Poland, is the oldest recorded Eucharistic miracle in the country. It involves the miraculous preservation of three consecrated Hosts that were desecrated, subsequently floating in mid-air in a marsh, and performing a healing miracle.

The Event

After being stolen and pierced, the three Hosts bled, and the blood miraculously healed a blind girl's sight. The desecrators threw the Hosts into a marsh, where a shepherd later witnessed them levitating in mid-air and emitting powerful flashes of light.

Legacy

The Hosts were recovered by the Bishop after they descended into his pyx. King Wladyslaw Jagiello ordered the construction of the Corpus Christi Church at the site where the floating Hosts were found. Additionally, the Church of the Most Sacred Blood of Jesus was later built over the site of the original desecration. This event remains a foundational pillar of Polish religious heritage.
