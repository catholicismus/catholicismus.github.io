Patierno, 1772

← Back to Miracles

The 1772 Eucharistic Miracle of Patierno, Naples, refers to the miraculous preservation of stolen consecrated Hosts, which were discovered perfectly intact despite being buried under manure.

The Event

After being stolen from the Church of St. Peter in 1772, the Hosts were found buried in a field. Witnesses were drawn to the site by mysterious rays of light and a hovering dove. Despite the burial conditions, the Hosts remained bright, undamaged, and free from any decay.

Church Approval and Scientific Inquiry

Scientific Consensus: Renowned anatomist Dr. Domenico Cotugno and later Professor Pietro De Franciscis confirmed that the perfect preservation of the Hosts defied all known physical principles.

Theological Significance: Saint Alphonsus Liguori wrote extensively about this event to deepen the faithful's devotion to the Real Presence of Christ in the Eucharist.

Modern Legacy

Although the original relics were stolen in 1978 and never recovered, the Church of St. Peter in Patierno remains an active and significant diocesan Eucharistic Sanctuary, commemorating the historical miracle.
