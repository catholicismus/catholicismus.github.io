Meerssen, 1222 & 1465

← Back to Miracles

The Eucharistic miracles in Meerssen (Netherlands) span two distinct, authenticated events. In 1222, living Blood dripped from a large Host during Sunday Mass and stained the altar corporal. In 1465, a brave peasant rescued this relic from a massive fire that destroyed the church.

The 1222 Miracle

During the celebration of Sunday Mass, just after the consecration, the priest experienced living Blood dripping from the large Host he was holding. The Blood fell onto and stained the corporal (the cloth placed on the altar). News of the event spread quickly, drawing flocks of pilgrims.

The 1465 Fire Miracle

In 1465, forces loyal to Duke Philip the Good of Burgundy attacked the town of Meerssen and burned down the Benedictine church. During the conflagration, a local peasant managed to run into the burning building and rescue the blood-stained Host and corporal. This event is locally celebrated as the "Miracle of the Fire."

Legacy and Location

Basilica Status: In 1938, Pope Pius XI elevated the church to a Minor Basilica. Today, pilgrims can visit the designated location at the Basilica of the Blessed Sacrament in Meerssen, Netherlands. The surviving sacred relics continue to be venerated, with a special procession held each year during the Octave of Corpus Christi.
