Augsburg, 1194

← Back to Miracles

The 1194 Eucharistic Miracle of Augsburg (also known as the "Miraculous Good") occurred when a woman took a consecrated Host home and preserved it in wax for five years. Upon opening the container in 1199, a priest found the Host transformed into living, bleeding flesh.

The Sequence of Events

  • The Concealment (1194): A woman from Augsburg, Germany, deeply devoted to the Eucharist, hid a consecrated Host in a handkerchief during Mass. She took it home and stored it in a wax container inside a cupboard.
  • The Confession (1199): Tormented by guilt, the woman confessed her action on May 11, 1199, to Father Berthold of the Holy Cross convent.
  • The Transformation: When the priest retrieved and opened the wax covering, he discovered that the Host had turned into bleeding flesh, with the two parts joined by thin threads of red flesh.
  • The Growth: The local bishop placed the host in a crystal monstrance for public veneration. A second phenomenon occurred: the Host began to swell and grow in size, cracking its wax casing, an event witnessed from Easter until the Feast of St. John the Baptist.

Current Status and Observance

Where to See It: The miraculous Host and the preserved pieces of wax remain intact today and are kept under glass at the Catholic Holy Cross Church (Heilig Kreuz) in Augsburg.

Annual Celebration: The parish commemorates this event annually on May 11.
