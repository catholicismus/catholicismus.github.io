Bagno di Romagna, 1412

← Back to Miracles

The 1412 Eucharistic Miracle of Bagno di Romagna occurred at the local Basilica of Santa Maria Assunta. During Mass, Father Lazzaro da Verona was assailed by doubts about the Real Presence of Christ. Upon consecration, the wine transformed into visible, living blood, boiling over the chalice and staining the linen corporal.

Key Details & Location

  • Location: The relic is kept in the Basilica di Santa Maria Assunta, located at Via Fiorentina, 40, 47021 Bagno di Romagna (FC), Italy.
  • The Relic: The blood-stained corporal is preserved in a dedicated chapel within the basilica.
  • Historical Investigation: Subsequent investigations and scientific analyses have confirmed the nature of the stains as human blood.

Viewing Information

The sacred linen cloth is traditionally displayed to the public during the Solemnity of Corpus Christi and on Sundays from spring through autumn, allowing pilgrims to personally venerate this divine sign.
