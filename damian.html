St. Peter Damian, 1050

← Back to Miracles

The 1050 Eucharistic Miracle of St. Peter Damian stands as a significant event in Church history, as it was directly witnessed and recorded by St. Peter Damian himself in his work Opusculum XXXIV.

The Miraculous Event

A young woman, influenced by a sorceress, attempted to steal a consecrated Host during Mass to use for witchcraft. When the priest confronted her and demanded she hand it over, they discovered that the Host had miraculously transformed: exactly half of it had turned into bleeding human flesh, while the other half remained in its original form as altar bread.

Theological Significance

St. Peter Damian, later declared a Doctor of the Church, noted that this miracle served as a divine intervention to combat 11th-century heresies that questioned the Real Presence of Christ. The visible blending of flesh and bread served as an indisputable testimony to the truth of Transubstantiation.
