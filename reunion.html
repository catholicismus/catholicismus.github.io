Saint-André de la Réunion, 1902

← Back to Miracles

The 1902 Eucharistic miracle of Saint-André de la Réunion occurred during the Forty Hours devotion, when thousands of witnesses saw the sorrowful face of Jesus Christ appear within the consecrated Host.

The Miraculous Manifestation

During public adoration, Abbot Henry Lacombe and thousands of parishioners witnessed a distinct, sorrowful face of Jesus with a crown of thorns appearing inside the monstrance. To ensure it was not an optical illusion, the pastor extinguished the lights and closed the shutters, yet the image became even clearer. Over several hours, the image transformed from the face of Jesus into a large crucifix before finally fading away.

Legacy

Abbot Lacombe meticulously documented the event, testifying to it at the 1904 Eucharistic Congress. This miracle is widely recognized and cataloged in the international exhibition of Eucharistic Miracles originally assembled by Blessed Carlo Acutis, serving as a profound witness to the Real Presence of Christ in the Eucharist.
