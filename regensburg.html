Regensburg, 1255

← Back to Miracles

The Eucharistic miracle of Regensburg (1255) occurred when a priest, troubled by doubts about the Real Presence, was interrupted during Mass by a wooden crucifix that came to life. The figure of Jesus extended its arms, took the chalice from the priest's hands, and held it up for the congregation to adore.

Key Background

  • The Origin (March 25, 1255): On Holy Thursday, a priest was bringing Viaticum (Communion) to a dying person. While crossing a swollen stream on a simple plank of wood, he slipped and dropped the ciborium containing the consecrated Hosts.
  • The Chapel (September 8, 1255): In reparation for the accident, the priest, locals, and civil authorities built a chapel on the exact site. It was consecrated by Bishop Albert.
  • The Miracle: Two years later, a priest celebrating Mass in this chapel suffered doubts during the Consecration. Unable to elevate the chalice, he hesitated, prompting the wooden crucifix above the altar to come to life. Jesus took the chalice, exhibited it to the faithful for adoration, and then handed it back to the repentant priest.
