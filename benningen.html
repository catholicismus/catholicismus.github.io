Benningen, 1216

← Back to Miracles

The 1216 Eucharistic Miracle of Benningen, Germany, occurred when a local miller stole a consecrated Host and hid it in a neighbor’s mill to frame him for theft and slander. The stolen Host subsequently began to bleed profusely, exposing the plot and leading to official Church recognition.

The Background

In the small village of Benningen, two millers had been locked in a bitter, long-standing feud. Exasperated by a continuous string of arguments, one of the millers devised a malicious plan.

The Stolen Host

After receiving Holy Communion during Mass, the disgruntled miller secretly removed the consecrated Host from his mouth and hid it among the stones of his rival’s mill to frame his neighbor for the crime of sacrilege.

The Miracle

On the Feast of St. Gregory, the hidden Host began to bleed so heavily that the miraculous event could not be ignored. The bleeding was so profuse that the news quickly spread throughout the entire village, ultimately reaching the local bishop.

Aftermath & Commemoration

The remorseful miller confessed his misdeed. The local Bishop of Augsburg, Friedrich, retrieved the Host and placed it in a precious container at the Church of St. Peter. To commemorate this event, the citizens of Benningen built a chapel in 1221 known as the Riedkapelle zum Hochwürdigen Gut. Today, the parish still celebrates the miracle every year during the Feast of Corpus Christi with a traditional procession.
