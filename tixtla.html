Tixtla, 2006

← Back to Miracles

The 2006 Eucharistic Miracle of Tixtla, Mexico, occurred on October 21, 2006, when a consecrated Host began to exude a reddish substance later scientifically confirmed to be living human heart tissue and blood.

The Scientific Findings

Led by Dr. Ricardo Castañón Gómez, the interdisciplinary team discovered:

  • Human Tissue: The substance was identified as living human cardiac muscle (myocardium) showing signs of severe physical trauma.
  • Authentic Blood: It was typed as AB positive, matching other historic Eucharistic miracles.
  • Internal Origin: Testing proved the blood originated from within the host, ruling out any external application.

Ecclesiastical Status

In 2013, Bishop Alejo Zavala Castro officially recognized the miracle, designating it as a "Divine Sign." The miracle is notably linked to the memory of Blessed Carlo Acutis, occurring just nine days after his passing, and is featured in his international exhibition.
