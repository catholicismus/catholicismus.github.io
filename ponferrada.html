Ponferrada, 1533

← Back to Miracles

The 1533 Eucharistic miracle of Ponferrada, Spain, involved consecrated Hosts that miraculously glowed with brilliant light after being stolen, compelling their recovery and revealing the Real Presence of Christ.

The Theft and Manifestation

A resident named Juan de Benavente stole a ciborium containing consecrated Hosts, intending to hide the evidence. However, the container became impossibly heavy, and he was forced to hide it in his home. Throughout the night, brilliant light radiated from the stolen Hosts, eventually leading to their abandonment in a blackberry patch. When recovered, they were found to be completely intact, defying the natural elements.

Legacy

The town of Ponferrada remembers this event through local landmarks and a commemorative painting housed in the New Church of St. Peter. This miracle stands as a historical witness to the sanctity of the Eucharist and the futility of sacrilege.
