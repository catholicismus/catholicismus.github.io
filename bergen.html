Bergen-op-Zoom, 1421

← Back to Miracles

The 1421 Eucharistic Miracle of Bergen (Bergen-op-Zoom, Netherlands) occurred after the pastor of the Church of Saints Peter and Paul, struggling with doubt regarding the Real Presence, discarded remaining consecrated Hosts into a river. Months later, local fishermen found the Hosts floating, miraculously stained with coagulated blood.

Key Historical Details

  • The Doubt: The parish priest had spent many months doubting the transubstantiation and the true presence of Christ in the Eucharist.
  • The Event: On the Sunday before Pentecost, he took the leftover consecrated Hosts from Mass and threw them into the local canal/river.
  • The Discovery: Several months later, local fishermen saw the Hosts floating in the water. They were visibly soaked in coagulated blood.

Legacy and Commemoration

News of the event spread rapidly, turning Bergen into a major destination for pilgrims and reigniting the faith of the local community. While the veneration was largely suppressed during the Protestant Reformation, local Catholics secretly kept the memory of the miracle alive for generations. In the 20th century, the devotion was officially restored.
