Veroli, 1570

← Back to Miracles

The 1570 Eucharistic miracle of Veroli, Italy, saw a series of miraculous visual manifestations of Christ and angels appearing inside a consecrated Host during public adoration at the Basilica of Sant'Erasmo.

The Event

On March 26, 1570, during a 40-hour period of public adoration, the Confraternity of Mercy witnessed a sequence of divine visions. These included a radiant star, the appearance of the Child Jesus, the scene of Jesus dying on the cross, and a final manifestation of angels surrounding the Sacred Host.

Documentation and Legacy

The miracle was formally recorded in a deposition preserved in the archives of the Church of St. Erasmus, with eyewitness testimony provided by Giacomo Meloni. While the original Host was consumed years later, the ceremonial silver chalice, paten, and pyx used in the 1570 adoration are still preserved as holy relics.

Commemoration: Every year, on the Tuesday after Easter, the town of Veroli celebrates this event with a solemn Mass and a procession using the original historical chalice.
