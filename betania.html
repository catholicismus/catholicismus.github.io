Betania, 1991

← Back to Miracles

The 1991 Eucharistic miracle of Betania, Venezuela, is a Vatican-recognized event where a consecrated Host bled spontaneously during midnight Mass, later confirmed by scientific analysis to be human blood type AB.

The Miraculous Event

On the Feast of the Immaculate Conception in 1991, Father Otty Ossa Aristizábal witnessed a red substance emanating from a consecrated Host. Laboratory tests in Caracas confirmed it was AB-type human blood, matching tissue from a living human heart under trauma. This was followed by a 1998 incident where a pilgrim captured video of the Host appearing to pulse like a beating heart.

Ecclesiastical Recognition

Bishop Pio Bello Ricardo officially declared the event a miracle of supernatural origin. Today, the miraculous Host is preserved for public veneration in the chapel of the Augustinian Recollect Nuns in Los Teques, serving as a powerful modern-day testament to the Real Presence of Christ.
