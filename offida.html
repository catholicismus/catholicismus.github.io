Offida, 1273

The Eucharistic Miracle of Offida took place in Lanciano in 1273, though the holy relics are preserved in the Church of St. Augustine in Offida, Italy. It serves as a profound testimony to the Real Presence of Christ.

The Sacred Narrative

A woman named Ricciarella, misguided by a witch, attempted to use a consecrated Host for a "love potion" by burning it. Instead of turning to ash, the Host transformed into living, bleeding human flesh. For seven years, the relic remained hidden in her stable, where the family mule famously knelt in reverence whenever it passed the concealed Host.

The Journey to Offida

In 1280, Ricciarella confessed her sacrilege to an Augustinian friar, Father Giacomo Diotallevi, who retrieved the miraculous relic. It was later sealed in a beautiful silver cross-shaped reliquary in Venice.

The Relic Today: The reliquary, containing the miraculous Host, the tile fragment, and a piece of the True Cross, is permanently displayed and venerated at the Church of St. Augustine in Offida.
