Neuvy-Saint-Sépulcre, 1257

← Back to Miracles

The 1257 "Eucharistic miracle" of Neuvy-Saint-Sépulcre, France, is unique. Instead of a bleeding host, it marks the arrival of two sacred drops of the Most Precious Blood of Jesus Christ, believed to have been collected on Calvary.

Key Historical Details

  • The Origin: The relic was brought from the Holy Land to France in 1257 by Cardinal Eudes.
  • The Location: It is housed in the Collegiate Church of Saint-Étienne, which was built in the 11th century and modeled directly after the Church of the Holy Sepulchre in Jerusalem.
  • Veneration: Pope Gregory XV granted indulgences to pilgrims devoted to the Precious Blood here in 1623.
  • Present Day: Pilgrims and the faithful continue to visit the church, with special Solemn Masses and processions held annually on Easter Monday and July 1st.
