Blanot, 1331

← Back to Miracles

The Eucharistic Miracle of Blanot occurred on Easter Sunday, March 31, 1331, in the village of Blanot, France. During Communion, a consecrated Host dropped by a parishioner transformed into visible blood and heart tissue.

The Timeline of Events

  • The Incident: During Easter Mass, vicar Hugues de la Baume distributed Communion. While a woman named Jacquette was receiving the Host, it fell onto a cloth covering her hands.
  • The Transformation: When alerted, the priest returned to retrieve it and found the Host had vanished, leaving only a bloodstained cloth.
  • Washing the Stain: The priest took the cloth to the sacristy and attempted to scrub the stain in a basin of clear water. Instead of washing away, the blood spot darkened and expanded to the exact shape and size of the Host, while the water in the basin turned bloody.

The Relics

The Cloth: The priest carefully cut the bloody imprint from the cloth, which was placed in the tabernacle alongside the remaining, perfectly preserved consecrated hosts.

Current Location: While the remaining Hosts were unfortunately lost during the French Revolution, the bloodstained cloth was hidden and preserved by a local parishioner.

Veneration: The relic is kept in St. Martin's Church in Blanot and is solemnly exposed every year on the feast of Corpus Christi.
