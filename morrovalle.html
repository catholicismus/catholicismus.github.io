Morrovalle, 1560

← Back to Miracles

The 1560 Eucharistic Miracle of Morrovalle, Italy, refers to the miraculous preservation of a consecrated Host from a devastating fire that completely destroyed a Franciscan church.

The Event

On April 17, 1560, a massive fire raged for seven hours, reducing the Franciscan church and convent to ruins. Ten days later, Father Battista da Ascoli discovered the pyx (sacred vessel) buried in the rubble. While the pyx was burned, the large consecrated Host inside remained completely intact, white, and undamaged.

Legacy

  • Papal Recognition: Pope Pius IV investigated the event and declared it supernatural, issuing a Papal Bull in 1560 to approve public devotion.
  • Civitas Eucaristica: In 1960, the Morrovalle City Council permanently inscribed "Civitas Eucaristica" (Eucharistic City) at the town's main gate.
  • The Relic Today: The preserved Host and the surviving pyx lid are housed and venerated in the Church of San Bartolomeo in Morrovalle.
