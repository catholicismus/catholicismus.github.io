Stiphout, 1342

← Back to Miracles

The 1342 Eucharistic miracle of Stiphout (Netherlands) refers to a miraculous event where consecrated hosts were entirely preserved from a massive church fire. It is one of the historically documented Eucharistic marvels promoted in the official exhibits of Blessed Carlo Acutis.

The Event

In 1342, a violent thunderstorm passed through the village of Stiphout, and a lightning strike set the parish church on fire. The blaze quickly raged out of control, destroying the entire building.

The Rescue

Unable to enter through the burning doors, a local man named Jan Baloys climbed onto a roof and was lowered down through a window near the altar. He found that the flames—which had already reduced the rest of the church to ashes—were miraculously kept completely away from the area surrounding the tabernacle.

Outcome & Legacy

Baloys successfully removed the ciborium containing the consecrated hosts. The parish church in Stiphout features a historical painting that depicts Jan Baloys rescuing the Blessed Sacrament from the flames. Local residents and the faithful continue to venerate this event, with special observances often held during the feast of Corpus Christi.
