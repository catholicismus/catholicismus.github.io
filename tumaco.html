Tumaco, 1906

← Back to Miracles

In 1906, after an 8.8 magnitude earthquake generated a devastating tsunami off the coast of Colombia, the coastal town of Tumaco was miraculously spared. When a massive wall of water threatened the island, parish priest Father Gerardo Larrondo held up the Blessed Sacrament, halting the deadly wave in its tracks.

The Miracle Details

  • The Date: January 31, 1906.
  • The Event: Following a 10-minute undersea earthquake, the ocean rapidly receded and a colossal tsunami wave formed, plunging toward the tiny barrier island of Tumaco.
  • The Action: Terrified townsfolk ran to their parish church. Father Larrondo consumed the small Hosts, leaving only the large Host in the monstrance. He led the weeping faithful in a Eucharistic procession to the shoreline.
  • The Deliverance: As the mountain of water approached, Father Larrondo raised the monstrance and traced the Sign of the Cross in the air. The towering wave abruptly stopped and receded immediately, sparing the island from the destruction that ravaged surrounding coastal areas.

Commemoration

Because of this historic deliverance, it is widely celebrated in Catholic devotion and is featured in the official exhibitions of Eucharistic miracles originally created by Blessed Carlo Acutis.
