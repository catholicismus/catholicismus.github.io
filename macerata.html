Macerata, 1356

← Back to Miracles

The Eucharistic Miracle of Macerata occurred on April 25, 1356, in Macerata, Italy, when a priest experiencing severe doubts about the Real Presence of Jesus in the Eucharist witnessed the consecrated Host bleed heavily during Mass.

The Event

The miracle took place in the chapel of the Church of St. Catherine. While breaking the bread before Holy Communion, a priest was filled with strong doubts regarding transubstantiation. At the moment he broke the Host, a large amount of fresh blood flowed from it, staining the altar cloth (corporal) and the chalice underneath.

Investigation and Relics

The Relic: The blood-stained corporal is preserved and can be venerated today under the altar of the Most Holy Sacrament in the Cathedral of Holy Mary Assumed and St. Giuliano.

Historical Record: A 14th-century parchment detailing the miracle is preserved in the church, and the cloth continues to be featured in an annual procession during the Feast of Corpus Christi.
