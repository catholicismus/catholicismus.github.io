Walldürn, 1330

← Back to Miracles

The 1330 Eucharistic miracle in Walldürn, Germany, occurred when Father Heinrich Otto accidentally spilled consecrated wine onto the altar cloth (corporal). The spilled blood miraculously formed the image of the crucified Christ, surrounded by 11 crowned heads, and has since drawn thousands of pilgrims to the region.

The Event

  • The Incident: While celebrating Mass, Father Heinrich Otto accidentally knocked over the chalice, spilling the consecrated wine onto the corporal.
  • The Manifestation: The spilled liquid formed a clear, lasting image of the Crucified Christ, surrounded by eleven thorn-crowned heads.
  • The Secrecy: Overcome with shame and fear, the priest hid the cloth inside a stone in the altar. He only revealed its location fifty years later on his deathbed.

Historical Recognition

Veneration & Papal Approval: Once the cloth was discovered, it caused a surge in devotion. In 1445, Pope Eugene IV officially recognized and authenticated the miracle.

Basilica: The church where the miracle took place, the Wallfahrtsbasilika St. Georg in Walldürn, Germany, was later elevated to a minor basilica.

Pilgrimage Today

The sacred corporal is still venerated in the Basilica of St. George today. The main period for the "Pilgrimage to the Holy Blood" spans four weeks, beginning annually on Trinity Sunday.
