Canosio, 1630

← Back to Miracles

The Eucharistic Miracle of Canosio took place in 1630 in the small village of Canosio, Italy, located in the Maira Valley of the Piedmont region. The event is celebrated for saving the town from a devastating flood.

The Miracle

In 1630, the local Maira River swelled dangerously due to torrential rains, causing a massive mudslide and floodwaters to rush directly toward the village. Fearing total destruction, the local pastor, Father Francesco Reinardi, led a solemn procession to the oncoming flood. He stood before the waters, raised the Monstrance, and blessed the cresting river with the Blessed Sacrament. Instantly, the rain stopped and the floodwaters subsided, sparing Canosio.

Commemoration

  • The Monument: To honor the divine protection, the citizens of Canosio built a commemorative chapel on the site where Father Reinardi gave the Eucharistic blessing.
  • Annual Feast: Every year on the Feast of Corpus Christi, the townspeople continue to hold a solemn procession with the Eucharist to the chapel, renewing their gratitude.
  • Exhibition: This event is officially documented as part of the worldwide Eucharistic Miracles photographic exhibition originally created by Blessed Carlo Acutis.
