Salzano, 1517

← Back to Miracles

The 1517 Eucharistic Miracle of Salzano, near Venice, Italy, is a unique event where grazing animals demonstrated visible recognition of the Real Presence of Christ in the Eucharist.

The Miraculous Account

While Father Lorenzo was traveling to administer the Viaticum to a dying person, his path took him through meadows near the Muson River. As he passed by, grazing donkeys stopped, approached the priest, and knelt in genuflection before the Blessed Sacrament. The animals followed him to the sick person's home, knelt outside during the rite, and accompanied the priest part of the way back.

Historical Significance

This event serves as a striking testimony to the reverence due to the Blessed Sacrament. It is recognized in Church history and is featured as a significant historical event in the Eucharistic Miracles exhibition originally compiled by Blessed Carlo Acutis.
