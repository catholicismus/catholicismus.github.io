Zaragoza, 1427

← Back to Miracles

The 1427 Eucharistic miracle of Zaragoza, Spain, occurred when a stolen consecrated Host miraculously transformed into the living Baby Jesus, remaining unharmed despite attempts to destroy it by fire.

The Miraculous Transformation

A woman seeking a love potion stole a consecrated Host from the Church of Saint Michael. Upon opening the box where it was hidden, she and a sorcerer witnessed the Host transform into a beautiful, radiant Baby Jesus. Even when the woman attempted to burn the Host, the Baby remained vibrantly alive and unhurt, leading her to deep repentance and a public confession of her sacrilege.

Historical Legacy

This event was formally recorded by the archdeacon, Don Dorner. Today, the legal archives documenting the testimonies are held in the Zaragoza Town Hall, and a historical painting depicting the miracle is preserved in the Zaragoza Cathedral, serving as a powerful witness to the Real Presence of Christ in the Eucharist.
