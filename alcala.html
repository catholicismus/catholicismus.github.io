Alcalá de Henares, 1597

← Back to Miracles

The 1597 Eucharistic miracle of Alcalá de Henares, Spain, involved 24 consecrated Hosts that remained perfectly pristine and fresh for over a decade after being stolen, while control-group wafers placed in the same conditions rapidly decayed.

The Miracle of Preservation

After a repentant thief returned 24 stolen Hosts to the Jesuit Church, they were locked in a vault. Eleven years later, they were found to be completely unaffected by mold or decay. To verify the phenomenon, priests placed non-consecrated wafers in the same environment; these decayed quickly due to humidity, whereas the consecrated Hosts remained as fresh as the day they were returned.

Legacy

The preservation was officially declared a miracle in 1619. These Hosts were venerated for centuries before their disappearance during the chaos of the Spanish Civil War in 1936. Their legacy serves as a testament to the supernatural endurance of the Eucharist.
