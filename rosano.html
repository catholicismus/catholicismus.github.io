Rosano, 1948

← Back to Miracles

The 1948 miracle of Rosano, Italy, centers on a life-sized statue of the Sacred Heart of Jesus housed in a Benedictine monastery that miraculously wept and bled repeatedly over a period of two years.

The Miraculous Events

  • First Occurrence: On Easter Sunday, April 4, 1948, nuns and witnesses first observed blood-like droplets falling from the eyes of the statue.
  • Recurring Phenomena: Throughout 1948 and 1950, repeated episodes of bleeding were documented by the monastic community and pilgrims.
  • Investigation: The Holy Office conducted a formal investigation, led by Father Luigi Romoli, and the statue was eventually permitted to be returned to the monastery for veneration in 1952.

Legacy

The evidence, including medical confirmation of the blood and the cloths used to wipe the statue, is preserved in the monastery archives. The miracle deepened the spiritual commitment of the Benedictine community to their motto of Ora et Labora (Prayer and Work).
