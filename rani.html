Kanjikode, 1998

Miraculous Manifestation in Kerala

The Reported Miracle

On February 12, 1998, during a massive Bible convention in Kanjikode, Kerala, India, attended by approximately 100,000 people, an extraordinary phenomenon was reported. It was claimed that immediately after receiving Holy Communion, the consecrated Host transformed into bleeding flesh in the mouth of Rani John Palumparambil.

Witnesses and Observations

Several high-ranking clergy, including Archbishop Jacob Thoomkuzhy and Bishop Jacob Manathodath, were present and reportedly witnessed the physical changes of the Host inside her mouth. Alongside this, reports indicated the appearance of stigmata—wounds resembling those of Christ—on her hands and feet.

Documentation in the Compendium

As part of our record of reported phenomena, this event highlights the profound impact that such manifestations have on the faithful. While the Church maintains rigorous criteria for recognizing miraculous events, the account of Rani John remains a significant part of the recent history of Eucharistic devotion in the Kerala region, underscoring the deep faith of the community gathered in prayer.
