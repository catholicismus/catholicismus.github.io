Weiten-Raxendorf, 1411

← Back to Miracles

The 1411 Eucharistic miracle in Weiten-Raxendorf, Austria, involved a stolen consecrated Host. When the fleeing thief's horse inexplicably halted and later lunged, the Host fell to the ground unobserved. Days later, a woman found the broken Host radiating light, with the pieces joined by threads of bleeding flesh.

The Miracle Event

  • Theft: A thief stole a consecrated Host from the parish church in the village of Weiten, hid it in his glove, and fled on horseback.
  • The Halt: As the rider passed the Mühldorf valley (now known as "Am Schuß"), the horse abruptly stopped and refused to move, even after being struck.
  • The Drop: Without warning, the horse leaped into a gallop, causing the stolen Host to slip from the thief's glove and fall to the ground unnoticed.
  • Discovery: A few days later, a local woman named Mrs. Scheck from the nearby village of Mannersdorf found the Host encircled in a bright light. She discovered that the Host was broken in two but remained connected by bleeding threads of flesh.

Historical Impact & Location

The Shrine: To honor the event, Mrs. Scheck funded the construction of a chapel at the exact location the Host was found.

Pilgrimage: As word of the miracle spread, the site drew large crowds, eventually requiring the construction of a larger church to accommodate the pilgrims.

Location Details: The miracle is cataloged among the internationally recognized Eucharistic miracles. You can learn more or view the official documentation via the Miracoli Eucaristici Database.
