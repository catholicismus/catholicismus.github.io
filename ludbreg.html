Ludbreg, 1411

← Back to Miracles

The 1411 Eucharistic Miracle of Ludbreg, Croatia, occurred when a priest doubting the reality of transubstantiation saw consecrated wine transform into blood. Pope Leo X officially authorized its veneration. Today, the perfectly intact relic draws thousands of pilgrims annually during "Holy Sunday".

Key Event Details

  • The Incident: During Mass inside Count Batthyány's castle chapel, the presiding priest was struck with a sudden crisis of faith, doubting if the wine actually transformed into the blood of Christ.
  • The Miracle: The wine in the chalice visibly turned into liquid blood. Overcome with panic, the priest hid the blood-filled chalice behind the main altar and revealed the secret only on his deathbed.
  • Church Approval: Following investigations into the event, Pope Leo X issued a papal bull in the early 16th century permitting the veneration of the relic.

Veneration & Relic Today

The Monstrance: Since 1721, the miraculous relic has been safely housed in a beautiful, ornate monstrance commissioned from the Augsburg goldsmiths’ school.

Sveta Nedilja (Holy Sunday): The miracle is celebrated over an entire week at the beginning of September, drawing thousands of pilgrims from Croatia and neighboring countries to the Sanctuary of the Precious Blood of Christ.
