Trani, 11th Century

← Back to Miracles

The 11th-century Eucharistic Miracle of Trani, Italy, occurred when a consecrated Host, sacrilegiously fried in oil, miraculously transformed into bleeding human flesh, staining the home and pan.

The Miraculous Event

A skeptical woman stole a consecrated Host during Mass and threw it into a pan of boiling oil to test if it was truly the Body of Christ. Instantly, the Host transformed into bloody flesh, causing a massive hemorrhage that spilled over the pan and the room, shocking her and her neighbors who rushed to witness the event.

Legacy

Historical Records: The miracle is documented in texts such as the 17th-century Italia Sacra. In 1706, the site of the desecration was converted into a chapel.

The Relic: Today, the relic is preserved in the Cathedral of Holy Mary of the Assumption in Trani. St. Padre Pio famously remarked that Trani is doubly blessed, having been "bathed by the Blood of Christ."
