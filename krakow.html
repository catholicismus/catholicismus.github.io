Kraków, 1345

The 1345 Eucharistic Miracle of Kraków, Poland, occurred when stolen consecrated Hosts, abandoned in a muddy swamp, emitted a brilliant, supernatural light that guided the faithful to their recovery.

The Miraculous Light

After thieves discarded a stolen monstrance containing the Blessed Sacrament into the marshes of Kazimierz, the area was illuminated by intense, blinding flashes of light. This light was so radiant that it was visible for kilometers, continuing uninterrupted for several days and acting as a divine beacon for the local bishop and townspeople.

Legacy

The Hosts were recovered perfectly intact and dry, despite being in the swamp. To honor this event, King Casimir III the Great ordered the construction of the Corpus Christi Basilica on the site of the miracle, which remains a prominent historical and religious landmark in Kraków today.
